Industry Economics

World Cup Revenue Surge Hits Canadian Host Cities

Canadian hotels in FIFA World Cup host cities are capturing significant premiums. Toronto saw ADR lift 47.6% and RevPAR increase 36.2% around its June 12 match. Vancouver recorded even stronger gains with ADR jumping 54.0% and RevPAR up 30.4% for its June 13 fixture.

However, Vancouver's booking status as of June 15 showed occupancy at 64% for the June 24 match, down 8.6% year-over-year, suggesting demand may be softer for later-stage games or pricing is pushing some travelers to alternatives.

DMO Implication: Host city DMOs should be documenting these premiums for future mega-event bids. Non-host destinations within driving distance have an opportunity to position as value alternatives.

Industry Economics

Canadian Grand Prix Calendar Shift Reshapes Quebec Performance

The Canadian Grand Prix's move from June to May dramatically boosted Quebec province performance. Montreal achieved 78.0% occupancy (+8.1% YoY), ADR of CAD$313.08 (+26.7%), and RevPAR of CAD$244.09 (+36.9%).

The province-wide impact was substantial: Quebec occupancy reached 72.1% (+5.9%), ADR hit CAD$276.62 (+20.0%), and RevPAR climbed to CAD$199.49 (+27.1%).

DMO Implication: Major event calendar changes create significant revenue shifts. DMOs should monitor event calendars regionally and model the impact of timing changes on their markets.

Confidence

SourceSTR via Hospitality Net, June 2026

Consumer Behaviour

Set-Jetting Goes Mainstream

Travel inspired by movies, TV series, and streaming content is now a dominant force in destination selection. According to Expedia's Unpack '26 trends report, 53% of travelers show growing interest in screen-inspired travel. Among Gen Z and Millennials, 81% cite movies and TV shows as influences on where they travel.

Popular examples include destinations made famous by Emily in Paris, The White Lotus, Game of Thrones, and The Lord of the Rings. The trend is fundamentally shifting how travelers discover destinations, with streaming platforms expanding global content reach.

DMO Implication: DMOs should audit their destination's screen presence and consider partnerships with film commissions. Destinations featured in content should develop themed packages, guided tours, and social media engagement strategies targeting younger demographics.

Confidence

SourceExpedia Unpack '26 via Hospitality Net

Sustainability

Sustainable Procurement Becomes Mandatory – And It Affects Canadian Exports

Regulatory pressure is forcing hospitality supply chain transformation. The EU's Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive (CSRD) now requires in-scope companies to report on supply chain environmental impact. The Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (CSDDD) mandates identification of adverse environmental and human rights impacts within supplier networks.

France's AGEC Law restricts single-use plastics in hospitality amenities and key cards. Singapore's Hotel Sustainability Roadmap targets emission and waste reductions by 2030. Travel surveys from 2023-2024 show sustainability credentials are increasingly cited as differentiators, particularly among guests aged 25-44.

Canadian operators looking to export through travel trade should pay close attention: travelers arriving from markets where these standards are mandatory will expect similar sustainability practices at Canadian properties. Their travel trade partners – tour operators, OTAs, and travel agencies in Europe and Asia – are increasingly required to verify supplier credentials as part of their own compliance obligations. A Canadian hotel or experience provider without documentation may find itself excluded from international distribution channels.

DMO Implication: This is no longer just about guest preferences – it's about market access. Supporting operator members in building documentation and verification capabilities is now a competitive necessity for inbound tourism.

Confidence

SourceHospitality Net, June 2026

Sustainability

Responsible Tourism Innovation in Mountain Destinations

Banff & Lake Louise Tourism is pioneering visitor management and sustainability programs that offer models for other destinations. "Park Mode" encourages responsible visitor behavior through activation events and rewards. "Trip Builder" is a digital planning tool that manages expectations and distributes visitor traffic more effectively.

"Cool 2 Carpool" at Mt. Norquay offers deeply discounted lift tickets to visitors who share rides. Results include: Pursuit Hotels invested nearly $350,000 in energy efficiency projects; Banff Borrows diverted 37,357 single-use items from waste at the 2025 Christmas Market; partnerships with Stoney Nakoda First Nations create Indigenous tourism career pathways.

DMO Implication: Visitor management tools and incentive programs can shift behavior at scale. DMOs facing capacity pressure should study the Banff model.

SourceBanff Lake Louise Tourism

Help your operators get export-ready on sustainability credentials

EU CSRD and CSDDD now require supply chain environmental reporting. France restricts single-use plastics. Singapore targets 2030 reductions. Position for rate growth, not just occupancy recovery

The Evidence

Canadian ADR grew +9.5% YoY while occupancy only moved +0.9%. The market has pricing power. US hotels showing similar dynamics with +3.4% ADR growth.

What Changes In Your Week
