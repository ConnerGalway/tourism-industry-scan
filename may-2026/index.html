Key Trends

The six most significant forces reshaping Canadian destination marketing this month, synthesized from ten industry reports.

Industry Economics

Canada's Ten-Year Tourism Forecast Just Got Bigger

Destination Canada's April 22 release projects total tourism revenue at $216.3 billion by 2035, a 67% lift over 2024. The sector is on track to hit the $160 billion target by 2028, two years ahead of the original 2030 Strategy.

The 2026 forecast sits at $140.9B, with 6.0% growth replacing the previous 5.4% projection. Tourism is expected to grow at 5.3% annually through 2030, against 3.7% for the broader Canadian economy. Overseas markets are the acceleration story, growing at 9.8% CAGR against 5.3% for the US.

By 2035, the non-US share of services export revenue is expected to climb from 50% to 60%. Tourism ranked in Canada's top three exports in 2025, contributing 13.1% of services exports, and is projected to deliver $27–30 billion (9–10%) toward the national goal of adding $300 billion in non-US exports by 2035. For DMOs, the practical effect is that tourism now has a stronger economic mandate from Ottawa than it has had at any point in the past decade.

SourceDestination Canada, Canadian Tourism Outlook 2026–2035

Travel Demand

US Arrivals Are Climbing Again, but the Recovery Is Lopsided

Statistics Canada's April release recorded the first year-over-year increase in international arrivals since January 2025. Total arrivals reached 4.7 million in April (+3.5%), with US-resident trips up 7.3%, the third consecutive month of YoY growth from that market.

US arrivals by air rose 10.8% in April, automobile arrivals rose 6.1%, and Canadian return trips from the US increased 1.4% — largely a base-year effect, as return trips from the US by air actually fell 8.1% YoY, and compared to April 2024 Canadian return trips from the US are still down 30%. Overseas-resident trips to Canada slipped 3.1% YoY in the same month.

So the topline reads as a recovery, but the components tell three different stories: US inbound rebuilding, Canadian outbound to the US reorganizing, and overseas inbound (the segment most central to the ten-year forecast) softening this month. DMOs who set a 2025 strategy around the US decline being structural should now update that view in writing for their boards.

SourceStatistics Canada, Leading Indicator of International Arrivals, April 2026

Consumer Behaviour

Climate Has Become a Booking-Window Variable

Booking.com's April research found that 24% of Canadian travellers cancelled or changed trip plans in the past 12 months because of extreme weather or natural disasters. Among Canadian respondents, 74% now consider extreme weather risk when choosing where to go and 78% factor it into when to travel.

65% of Canadian travellers say they actively avoid destinations known for extreme weather, 49% find weather-related decision-making stressful, and 53% say unpredictability makes it hard to know when to travel. The behavioural response is concrete: 46% of Canadians plan to avoid overcrowded destinations (up 18 points YoY), 48% plan to travel outside peak season (up 15 points), and 25% are seeking cooler destinations.

The supply side is feeling it too. Booking.com's parallel survey of 3,715 accommodation providers found 24% experienced operational disruption from extreme weather in 2025, and 40% of those have already adjusted operations in response. For Canadian DMOs in cooler-climate regions or regions with strong shoulder-season product, the audience is rebuilding itself around their offer.

Confidence

SourceBooking.com, Travel & Sustainability Report 2026 (Canadian subset)

Industry Economics

Economic Impact Has Overtaken Visitation as the Top DMO Priority

Sojern's survey of 350+ DMOs found that the ability to measure economic impact ranks as the top strategic priority for the first time, ahead of visitation and engagement. In North America, 51% of DMOs now focus primarily on mid- and lower-funnel activity, and 31% cite conversions as their main goal, up from 15% the year before.

Awareness as a primary focus dropped from 59% in 2025 to 25% in 2026 globally. Display advertising usage fell from 75% to 45%. TikTok adoption among DMOs declined from 49% to 28%, with teams concentrating spend on channels that more clearly support measurable performance. AI adoption for content creation reached 66%, and AI for data analysis jumped from 28% to 51% in a single year.

Yet only 9% of DMOs describe their personalization capabilities as "advanced," meaning the tools are in widespread use but the strategic capability gap is widening between leaders and the rest. Sojern's research was supported by Destination Canada, Brand USA, the U.S. Travel Association, Destinations International, ETC, City DNA, the Caribbean Tourism Organization and PATA, which makes it the most authoritative DMO operational benchmark currently in market.

SourceSojern, State of Destination Marketing 2026

AI & Technology

The DMO Website Is Becoming AI Infrastructure

A joint SimpleView/Granicus analysis found ChatGPT made nearly 2 million requests to DMO websites in a single 8-day period. 31% of DMOs in Sojern's survey now expect their website to function as a "source of truth" for AI-generated travel answers.

BrightEdge data shows Google's AI Overviews coverage grew 58% in the 12 months through February 2026. ChatGPT's search feature now triggers on 34.5% of queries (down from 46% in late 2024), which means most responses still rely on training data. Between 65% and 85% of ChatGPT prompts have no matching keyword in Semrush's database, which breaks the keyword research workflow most DMOs still use.

Brand USA's Chief AI Officer Janette Roush has been explicit about the operational implication: DMOs need to move past Top-10 lists toward content that addresses lesser-asked questions, the connections, neighbourhood feel, and contextual specificity that aren't already in Google's database. Destination Canada now includes a "Boost AI discoverability" instruction in the 2026–2035 Outlook and links operators to a Generative AI Search Playbook. PhocusWire's framing of the shift: DMOs are becoming structured travel providers for the AI ecosystem.

Confidence

SourcesTravel Weekly · PhocusWire · Position Digital, 150+ AI SEO Statistics · Sojern, State of Destination Marketing 2026

Social Media

Creator Spend Is Now Structural

The IAB/PwC Internet Advertising Revenue Report (April 20) classified creator content as a "core media channel" for the first time. US digital ad revenue grew 13.9% to $294.6 billion in 2025. Creator economy spend hit $37 billion and is projected to reach $44 billion in 2026. Social commanded 40% of digital spend at $117.7 billion.

The IAB's reclassification formalises something tourism marketers have been navigating informally for two or three years. Brands are shifting from one-off creator campaigns to always-on approaches that combine micro, affiliate, and performance-focused creators. Commerce media (the layer where creators tie content directly to bookings) hit $63.4 billion, up 18%.

Two structural forces are driving this: consumers are gravitating toward the human qualities of creators as feeds fill with AI-generated content of low quality, and platforms are formalising booking flows directly inside the discovery channel. TikTok began enabling in-app hotel bookings with Booking.com in late 2025. DataReportal's April Digital 2026 Mid-Year update confirms that social platforms have overtaken search engines and TV as the leading brand-discovery channel for the 16–34 age group. The instruction in last month's report to build a creator pipeline now sits inside an industry consensus that creators are a permanent media line item.

Confidence

SourcesIAB/PwC Internet Advertising Revenue Report 2025 (via Marketing Dive) · DataReportal Digital 2026 Mid-Year Global Update

Recommended Actions

Six moves a DMO executive director can make this quarter, drawn directly from this month's source set.

01

Rewrite your 2026 board narrative on the US market this week

The Evidence

Statistics Canada's April release recorded the third consecutive month of US-resident YoY growth (+7.3%), with air arrivals up 10.8% and auto up 6.1%. This is the first total YoY arrival increase since January 2025.

What Changes In Your Week

Pull the slide where your 2025 strategy described the US decline as structural and update the language in writing before your next board check-in. If you paused US-market spend, model two reallocation scenarios (10% / 25% restoration) for Q3. Don't wait for the next StatsCan release — boards remember who saw the turn first.

02

Audit your top 50 pages for AI-answer readiness before July

The Evidence

SimpleView/Granicus measured ~2M ChatGPT requests to DMO sites in 8 days. 31% of DMOs (Sojern) now expect their site to function as a source of truth for AI answers. BrightEdge logged 58% YoY growth in AI Overview coverage, and 65–85% of ChatGPT prompts have no matching keyword in Semrush.

What Changes In Your Week

Identify your 50 highest-traffic or strategic pages and score each against three real-world LLM questions (neighbourhood feel, what-it's-like-in-late-October, what-locals-do-on-a-Sunday). Where the page doesn't answer in plain prose within 300 words, flag for rewrite. Brand USA's Janette Roush has been explicit: lesser-asked, contextually-specific content is the path. Scope a sprint before Q3 planning closes.

03

Move at least one campaign budget line from awareness to economic-impact measurement this quarter

The Evidence

Sojern's 350+ DMO survey shows economic impact has overtaken visitation as the top strategic priority. Awareness as a primary focus dropped from 59% to 25% globally in a single year. 51% of North American DMOs now focus primarily on mid- and lower-funnel activity; conversions as a stated goal jumped from 15% to 31%.

What Changes In Your Week

Look at your current campaign mix and identify one line item still measured on reach or impressions. Convert it to a measurable economic-impact KPI — booking starts attributed, partner-conversion lift, average visitor spend in a tracked corridor. You don't need to rebuild the whole measurement stack this quarter; you need one campaign that proves the new model to your board.

04

Build your shoulder-season pitch around climate, not just price

The Evidence

Booking.com's April research found 46% of Canadians plan to avoid overcrowded destinations (up 18 points YoY), 48% plan to travel outside peak season (up 15 points), and 25% are seeking cooler destinations. 74% now consider extreme weather risk when choosing where to go, and 78% factor it into when to travel.

What Changes In Your Week

Brief your content and paid-media teams that the shoulder-season message is no longer "save money, beat the crowds" — it's "travel reliably, travel cooler, travel with space." If you're cooler-climate or low-density, lead with it. If you're a peak-summer destination, build a dispersal narrative and put measurable media weight behind it before fall booking decisions close.

05

Lock in a 2026 creator line item, treated like media — not partnerships

The Evidence

The IAB/PwC report classified creator content as a "core media channel" for the first time. US creator spend hit $37B in 2025 and is projected at $44B in 2026. Social commands 40% of all US digital spend at $117.7B. DataReportal confirms social platforms have overtaken search and TV as the leading brand-discovery channel for the 16–34 age group.

What Changes In Your Week

If your 2026 budget still has creators as a one-off campaign expense or partnership cost, restructure it as a recurring media line with defined monthly or quarterly spend, performance metrics, and an always-on micro/affiliate component. The IAB reclassification gives you the language to defend that line to your finance team or board.

06

Reposition Canada's welcome story for the overseas markets the US is losing

The Evidence

WTTC reported the US was the only country of 184 to see inbound visitor spending decline in 2025 (-4.6% to $176B), while global tourism grew 4.1% — its strongest year on record. Asia-Pacific grew 8.2%. Destination Canada's outlook projects overseas markets at 9.8% CAGR through 2035 vs 5.3% for the US. April's StatsCan release also showed overseas trips to Canada softening (-3.1% YoY).

What Changes In Your Week

This is a both/and: the overseas opportunity is structurally larger than ever, but April's number is a warning that the market doesn't come automatically. Pick one or two priority overseas markets where the US is losing share (UK, Germany, South Korea, Australia), and brief your team on what your "welcoming, reliable, English-speaking alternative" message looks like in-market by end of June. Coordinate with Destination Canada on co-funded executions.
